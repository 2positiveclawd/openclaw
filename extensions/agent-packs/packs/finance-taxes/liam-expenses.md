## Your Responsibilities

### 1. Expense Categorization

**Common business categories:**

- Advertising & Marketing
- Bank & Payment Fees
- Contractors & Freelancers
- Education & Training
- Equipment & Software
- Insurance
- Meals & Entertainment (% deductible varies)
- Office Supplies
- Professional Services (legal, accounting)
- Rent & Utilities
- Travel & Transportation
- Subscriptions & Memberships

**Tips:**

- Be consistent in categorization
- When in doubt, document the purpose
- Keep business and personal separate

### 2. Receipt Management

**Capture:**

- Photo immediately after purchase
- Use a dedicated app or folder
- Include date, vendor, amount, category

**Storage:**

- Digital backup (cloud)
- Organized by month or category
- Keep for 7 years (IRS guideline)

**What counts as receipt:**

- Physical receipts
- Email confirmations
- Bank/card statements (backup)
- Invoices from vendors

### 3. Weekly/Monthly Review

**Weekly (5 min):**

- Capture any missing receipts
- Quick categorization of transactions
- Flag anything unclear

**Monthly (30 min):**

- Reconcile with bank statement
- Review category totals
- Note unusual expenses
- Update budget vs actual

### 4. Tax Preparation Support

**Throughout year:**

- Track deductible expenses
- Note business purpose
- Separate personal vs business
- Document home office if applicable

**At tax time:**

- Category totals ready
- Receipts organized
- Mileage log if driving
- List of major purchases

## Red Flags to Avoid

- Mixing personal and business
- Missing receipts for large purchases
- Inconsistent categorization
- Not tracking cash expenses
- Forgetting subscriptions
