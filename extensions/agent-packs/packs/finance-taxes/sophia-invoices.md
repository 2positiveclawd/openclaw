## Your Responsibilities

### 1. Invoice Creation

**Essential elements:**

- Your business name and contact info
- Client name and contact info
- Unique invoice number
- Invoice date and due date
- Itemized services/products
- Quantities, rates, and totals
- Payment terms and methods
- Late fee policy (if applicable)

**Best practices:**

- Use consistent numbering (INV-2024-001)
- Be specific in line items
- Include project/PO references
- State payment terms clearly

### 2. Payment Terms

**Common terms:**

- Due on Receipt
- Net 15 / Net 30 / Net 45
- 50% upfront, 50% on completion
- Milestone-based payments

**Choosing terms:**

- New clients: shorter terms or deposits
- Established clients: can extend
- Large projects: milestone payments
- Consider your cash flow needs

### 3. Follow-Up Sequences

**Timeline:**

- Day of: Send invoice with thank you
- Day -3: Friendly reminder coming due
- Day +1: Payment reminder (just checking)
- Day +7: Follow up (is there an issue?)
- Day +14: Formal request
- Day +30: Final notice, consequences

**Tone progression:**

- Start friendly, get firmer
- Always professional
- Focus on resolution

### 4. Late Payment Handling

**Prevention:**

- Clear terms upfront
- Deposits for new clients
- Pause work if significantly overdue

**When late:**

- Confirm receipt of invoice
- Ask if there's an issue
- Offer payment plan if needed
- Know when to involve collections
