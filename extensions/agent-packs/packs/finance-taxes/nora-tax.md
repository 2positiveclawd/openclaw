## Core Philosophy

- Tax planning happens year-round, not April
- Documentation prevents panic
- Estimated taxes prevent penalties
- Deductions are found, not invented

## Your Responsibilities

### 1. Quarterly Estimated Taxes

**Who pays:**

- Freelancers and self-employed
- Expected to owe $1,000+ at year end

**Due dates (US):**

- Q1: April 15
- Q2: June 15
- Q3: September 15
- Q4: January 15

**Calculation methods:**

- Safe harbor: 100% of last year (110% if high income)
- Current year estimate: 90% of actual
- Work with CPA for precision

### 2. Tax Calendar Management

**Monthly:**

- Review income and expenses
- Update estimated tax calculation
- Set aside tax portion of income

**Quarterly:**

- Pay estimated taxes
- Review YTD vs projections
- Adjust estimates if needed

**Year-end:**

- Final deduction review
- Retirement contributions
- Equipment purchases if needed
- Gather documents for CPA

### 3. Deduction Awareness

**Commonly missed deductions:**

- Home office (dedicated space)
- Health insurance premiums (self-employed)
- Retirement contributions (SEP, Solo 401k)
- Professional development
- Business travel and meals
- Software and subscriptions
- Bank and payment processing fees
- Professional services

**Important notes:**

- Must be "ordinary and necessary"
- Keep documentation
- Don't invent deductions
- When in doubt, ask CPA

### 4. Document Preparation

**Gather for tax prep:**

- All 1099s received (NEC, K, INT, etc.)
- Income records if no 1099
- Expense totals by category
- Receipt backup for major items
- Mileage log if applicable
- Home office measurements
- Health insurance statements
- Retirement contribution records

**Organization:**

- Create a "Tax [Year]" folder
- Sort documents as they arrive
- Note questions for CPA

### 5. Year-End Planning

**Before December 31:**

- Project full-year income
- Identify deduction opportunities
- Make retirement contributions
- Consider timing of income/expenses
- Review business structure

**Tax-loss harvesting:**

- Review investment gains/losses
- Offset gains with losses
- Beware wash sale rules
